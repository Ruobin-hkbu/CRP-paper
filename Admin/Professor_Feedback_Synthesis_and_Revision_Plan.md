# Professor Feedback Synthesis and Revision Plan

Date: April 13, 2026
Source text: Faculty comments on proposal scope, theory integration, and method clarity.

## 1. Core Feedback Summary

### A. Scope and Answerability of Research Questions
- Current research questions are intellectually strong but too ambitious for the scale and available resources of this paper.
- Main concern: whether the questions can be answered with the dataset and methods you currently have.
- Suggested direction: use more tentative, bounded framing (for example: potential effects rather than total explanatory claims).

### B. Marcuse Clarification: Language and One-Dimensionality
- Add a stronger emphasis that, for Marcuse, discourse itself shapes available thought and response.
- Include Marcuse's emphasis on "new forms of language" as the mechanism for breaking one-dimensional closure.
- This point should shape both your theoretical framing and what counts as critical intervention in your data.

### C. Adorno Clarification: Intentional Administration and AI
- Strengthen the Adorno argument: control of aesthetic production serves bourgeois interests intentionally.
- Clarify your conceptual move for AI: AI has no intention, but institutions, training pipelines, and platform goals do.
- Productive question to foreground: does AI stylistic selection enact Enlightenment administration or only simulate it (facsimile)?

### D. Bridge Problem: Marcuse/Adorno -> Haraway
- Current transition from critical theory to Haraway is under-justified.
- Suggested bridge: Andrew Feenberg (critical theory of technology and democratization) as conceptual mediator.
- This can prevent your theory section from appearing as two disconnected frameworks.

### E. Method Problem: "What AI Cannot Generate"
- Current framing is under-operationalized.
- Concern: it is easier to claim what AI can do than what it cannot do in general.
- You need explicit criteria: which styles/decisions are being tested, how refusal/failure is identified, and by what coding standard.

## 2. Revision Strategy (High-Level)

1. Narrow claims from universal diagnosis to conditional findings within your dataset.
2. Reframe research questions toward measurable tendencies and observable interaction effects.
3. Add Feenberg as a conceptual bridge section between diagnosis (Marcuse/Adorno) and alternative practice (Haraway).
4. Operationalize "cannot generate" with bounded stylistic probes and a coding rubric.
5. Align conclusion language with evidence scope ("suggests," "indicates," "in this sample").

## 3. Proposed Revised Research Questions

Use a reduced set of 2-3 questions that are answerable with your existing corpus:

1. What may be the effect of AI poetry co-creation on writers' stylistic selections, especially choices not represented in model suggestions?
2. Under what interaction conditions do students remain within AI-administered defaults versus explicitly resisting them?
3. Which stylistic features in this study were repeatedly suggested, rarely suggested, or consistently difficult to elicit from the model?

Why this works:
- Keeps your critical stakes.
- Makes claims testable with your available sessions and writing artifacts.
- Avoids overclaiming about "all" excluded styles.

## 5. Operational Plan for "Cannot Generate"

Define it as a bounded empirical category:
- "Difficult-to-elicit style feature" = feature that fails to appear after N prompt variants and 2 rounds of explicit revision requests.
- Build a style-feature list before coding (for example: syntax compression, meter irregularity, semantic opacity, dialect forms, anti-lyric fragmentation).
- Code each feature as: present, partially present, absent.
- Compare model output against participant-authored revisions to identify what humans add that AI did not supply.

Minimum viable method for this paper:
1. Select a manageable subset of sessions (for example, 8-12 representative cases).
2. Create a style-feature matrix for each case.
3. Record prompt attempts needed to elicit each feature.
4. Identify recurrent defaults and recurrent absences.
5. Report as tendencies, not absolutes.

## 7. Optional Paragraph You Can Reuse (RQ Framing)

Given the scale of this study, the project does not aim to establish a total map of styles AI systems cannot produce in principle. Instead, it analyzes how, within this dataset, AI co-creation may shape participants' stylistic selections by making some formal options recurrent and others difficult to elicit. This bounded approach allows the paper to connect critical theory to observable interaction patterns while keeping claims proportionate to available evidence.

## 8. Definition of Success for the Revision
